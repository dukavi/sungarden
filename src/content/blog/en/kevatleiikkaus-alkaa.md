Late February, early March — once temperatures no longer drop below -10 °C, it is time to start spring pruning of apple trees. In severe frost the branches are brittle and cuts do not heal properly, but in the milder late-winter conditions the work goes well.

![Pruning tools in the snow](../../../images/20260228_105612.jpg)

## Why is pruning important?

The goal of pruning is to keep the crop close to the central leader. When fruit grows near the trunk it receives more light and nutrients — and quality improves. At the same time, pruning prepares next year's harvest: new flower buds form on this summer's growth, and a properly pruned tree produces more consistently year after year.

## Tall spindle pruning

In an intensive apple orchard we use the tall spindle system. The principle is fairly simple:

- **Thick branches** are removed completely flush with the trunk
- **Medium branches** are cut to a short stub — a new, weaker shoot will grow from it this summer, which bears fruit better
- **Canopy width** is kept in check: any branches extending sideways are shortened so the canopy does not exceed the set width
- **Forks** are simplified — if a branch splits in different directions, only one direction is kept

![Apple tree before pruning in the winter orchard](../../../images/20260228_134952.jpg)

This keeps the tree narrow and full of light so every apple gets sunshine to ripen perfectly. Spring pruning is one of the most important jobs in the orchard — and it is happening right now in Mustio.
