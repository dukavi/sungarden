Winter bench grafting is one of the most efficient ways to produce new apple tree seedlings. The work is done indoors at a bench while rootstocks and scion wood are in winter dormancy.

## Timing

In theory, you can start as early as January, but that requires careful storage at 0...+2 °C for several months. We graft in early spring, in March, so that the storage period stays short and the grafts can go into the ground soon.

## Rootstocks

![Rootstock bundles ready for grafting](../../../images/20260311_115512.jpg)

We use dwarfing and semi-dwarfing rootstocks. They arrive in bundles and are kept moist and cool before grafting. A few days before grafting, we bring the rootstocks into a warm room — this wakes up the cambium layer and improves grafting success.

## Grafting

When the rootstock and scion are roughly the same thickness, we use the whip-and-tongue technique: both receive matching diagonal cuts with interlocking tongues that hold them tightly together. If the scion is noticeably thinner than the rootstock, we graft without the tongue — a simple whip graft is enough, as long as the cambium layers align on at least one side.

![Graft wrapped with rubber strip](../../../images/20260315_211645.jpg)

For wrapping, we use rubber strips. This method is practical because the rubber presses the scion and rootstock firmly together, ensuring good cambium contact. The rubber doesn't need to be removed — it degrades on its own as the plant grows.

## Waxing

![Waxed graft union](../../../images/20260315_211657.jpg)

After wrapping, the graft union is dipped in melted wax mixture. The wax protects the joint from drying out and from pathogens. We use a wax-water mixture that forms a thin protective film.

## Stratification

![Grafts stratifying in moist peat](../../../images/20260316_200350.jpg)

The waxed grafts are placed in boxes and covered with moist peat or sawdust. The boxes are kept warm (+18...+22 °C) for 7–10 days. During this time, callus begins to form at the graft union — tissue that bonds the rootstock and scion together.

![Grafts after stratification](../../../images/20260318_122237.jpg)

## Storage and planting

After stratification, the grafts are moved to cool storage (0...+2 °C) where they wait for planting. Planting time is late April to May, when the ground has thawed and night frosts have eased.
